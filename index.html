Grameen Bank

is a global non-profit organization dedicated to helping entrepreneurial women who live in poverty build businesses to enable financial mobility. Founded in Queens in 2008, this micro-finance-nonprofit organization is based on the Nobel Prize-winning of Muhammad Yunus' Grameen Bank. We are showing different groups of people that have benefitted from Grameen Bank in order to better understand how participants are similar and different.

The data on loan accounts from Grameen America was filtered to remove non-categorical columns. One-hot encoding of the remaining categories produced a sparse data set, and the dimensionality was reduced using principal component analysis. The resulting data set was grouped into five data sets using k-means clustering and compared to the original data for insight.
